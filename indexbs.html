A journey towards digital sovereignty

Published: 9th December 2024, 14:30

Last year, I grew tired of relying on faceless corporations to control my digital life. My data felt like it was floating aimlessly in the ether—perfectly good services would shut down without warning, prices would skyrocket once they had you hooked, and security breaches seemed all too common... who has time to wade through those endless privacy policies and T&Cs? The never-ending subscription fees felt like throwing away hard-earned money.

This spring, I decided to take matters into my own hands. I won an auction for a retired enterprise Dell PowerEdge R730 server—cheaper than an off-the-shelf NAS and far more powerful, albeit a bit noisier! Before long, I had fully committed to self-hosting, and it's been an incredibly rewarding journey ever since.

Now, I run a Proxmox server at home that virtualises as many linux servers as I need, and they host everything from my media library and network shares to a Git instance and workstation backups. It's become my digital fortress, and I absolutely love it.

What is this 'Digital Sovereignty'?

Digital sovereignty, in essence, is the notion of reclaiming control over your data and online experiences. Instead of relying on centralised services, you manage your own systems, retaining autonomy over your information and digital footprint. It's about knowing where your data resides, how it's being used, and taking agency over your technology. If you like to tinker, there's plenty to keep you entertained, and if you like things simple, well, stick to the default settings!

The Rewards of Self-Hosting

Let's talk about why self-hosting is so appealing.

Financial Freedom: Ditching the Subscriptions

One of the most tangible benefits is the cost savings. I was tired of paying monthly for cloud storage, media streaming, and other services. With self-hosting, I've replaced many of these with fantastic free and open-source alternatives. Instead of paying for cloud storage, for example, I now use Nextcloud to manage my files on my own server. The savings can add up significantly over time. Sure, in my case, I did make some upfront purchases, but I did the maths, it will pay for itself in 12 months, and then I'm reaping rewards. Of course, you don't need fancy hardware, you can run a server on an old laptop, or salvaged e-waste destined for landfill, just be sure you factor in energy costs to your calculations when choosing old hardware, as it can be less efficient, but old hardware can still make sense, so don't discount it.

Enhanced Privacy and Control: Your Data, Your Rules

Beyond cost savings, the enhanced control over my data is invaluable. I decide how I store my information and who has access to it, how long I keep it for, and there's no risk of losing it when the corporation decides to pull the plug. I don't have to worry about privacy breaches with large corporations. For example, using Jellyfin for media streaming provides a private and customisable experience compared to commercial services. If I want to share my files outside my home network, I can setup a reverse proxy with WireGuard and tunnel in securely, reaching my files from anywhere on the planet, and share them with whom I choose! I don't have to follow arbitrary rules on how many users can access my services, and no one is hoovering up data on my hobbies and interests.

An Endless Learning Opportunity: Skills that Matter

Self-hosting isn't just about saving money; it's about learning. Setting up my own servers has been an education in itself. I've gained invaluable skills in command line, UNIX, networking, system administration, security and firewalls, and configuration management. These are skills that translate well into many areas of technology, and I love the challenge of learning more every single day, because there's always something to add to my swiss army knife of services.

Taking Back Your Digital Life

Self-hosting isn't a gimmick; it's a path to a more independent and empowered digital life. By diving in, you can achieve digital sovereignty, reduce recurring costs, and acquire valuable skills. You have the power to take control of your data, learn about the technology you depend on, and break free from the grip of centralised services.

Are you ready to start exploring the world of self-hosting? Take back control, build new skills, and start your journey today.
